# NEXUS v2 Presenter Deck Plan

Audience:
- Business and leadership
- Product
- Engineering
- Triage / operations
- Prospective customers

Objective:
- Give a concise, credible 4-slide pitch that explains the problem, the product, the live UI proof, and the business direction.
- Keep it presentation-friendly for a presenter who needs to speak confidently without reading the full walkthrough.

Narrative arc:
1. The problem: incident intake is fragmented, opaque, and slow.
2. The solution: NEXUS turns incident work into a queue-first operating model with a clear console and controlled path to action.
3. The proof: the current UI already shows queue, incident console, intake, replay, training, and trust posture.
4. The business case: the product is positioned for product credibility now and deeper backend hardening next.

Slide list:
1. Title and problem statement
2. Product solution and operating model
3. Live product proof with screenshots
4. Business value, rollout, and next phase

Source plan:
- README.md for the product story and current status language
- docs/DEMO_WALKTHROUGH.md for the detailed end-to-end flow
- docs/LIVE_DEMO_SPEAKER_NOTES.md for speaker phrasing
- live UI screenshots from the local product for the proof slide

Visual system:
- Dark enterprise palette with deep navy background and bright cyan/green accents
- Clean sans typography
- Large title blocks, concise body text, and structured cards
- Product screenshots used as evidence panels rather than decorative clutter

Asset needs:
- Queue screenshot
- Incident console screenshot
- Inputs screenshot
- Replay or settings/training screenshot

Editability plan:
- Titles, body copy, labels, and speaker notes remain editable PowerPoint text.
- Screenshots are embedded as image assets.
- No important message is locked into images except the live UI proof itself.

Presenter emphasis:
- Why the problem matters
- Why the queue-first model is better
- Why the UI already looks enterprise-grade
- Why the current phase is credible for demos and stakeholder review
